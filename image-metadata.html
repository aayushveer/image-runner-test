What is Image Metadata?

Image metadata is hidden information embedded within digital photos. When you take a photo with a camera or smartphone, it automatically stores data like the camera model, date/time, GPS location, camera settings (aperture, shutter speed, ISO), and sometimes even the software used to edit it.

This data can be useful for organizing photos, but it can also be a privacy concern. When you share photos online, this hidden data can reveal your location, device information, and personal details.
