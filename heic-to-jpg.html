Frequently Asked Questions

What is HEIC format and why can't I open it on Windows?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's default photo format since iOS 11. It offers 50% smaller file sizes than JPEG with same quality. Windows doesn't natively support HEIC, so you need to convert it to JPG or install HEIC codec. Our free converter instantly converts HEIC to JPG without any software installation.

How to convert HEIC to JPG on iPhone?

You can change iPhone settings to capture in JPG (Settings > Camera > Formats > Most Compatible) or use our online converter. Just visit imgrunner.com/heic-to-jpg, select your HEIC files, and download as JPG instantly. Works on iPhone Safari browser too.

Is it safe to convert HEIC files online?

What's the difference between HEIC and HEIF?

HEIF (High Efficiency Image Format) is the container format, while HEIC is specifically for images using HEVC compression. In practice, both .heic and .heif files work the same way. Our converter supports both formats.

Does converting HEIC to JPG reduce quality?

There's minimal quality loss when converting HEIC to JPG at high quality settings (85-100%). Our converter lets you adjust the quality slider to balance file size and image quality. For lossless conversion, choose PNG format.

Why are my iPhone photos in HEIC format?

Apple uses HEIC by default since iOS 11 because it saves 50% storage space compared to JPG while maintaining the same quality. This helps save space on your iPhone. You can change this in Settings > Camera > Formats > Most Compatible.
